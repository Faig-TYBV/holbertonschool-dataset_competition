The data shows a sudden acceleration in Air transport income starting in 2015. This aligns with the national "Strategic Roadmap for Logistics and Trade," which aimed to:
1. Diversify the Non-Oil Economy: Move away from the volatility of pipeline-dependent revenue.
2. Capitalize on the Middle Corridor: Position Baku as the primary "Air-Bridge" for time-sensitive, high-yield cargo (pharmaceuticals, electronics, e-commerce) between Asia and Europe.
3. Build Resilience: As seen in 2020, while land and sea routes are vulnerable to physical borders, a dominant Air sector provides a strategic "Open Door" ensuring continuous revenue flow during global crises.

#### Phase 2.5: The "Efficiency" Audit ‚Äì Revenue vs. GDP

At first glance, the data shows a massive upward trend in revenue. However, a responsible analyst must ask a difficult question: Is this income increasing simply because the country is getting richer (inflation/general growth), or is the sector becoming a true economic engine?

> "Earning more money does not always mean we are developing. We must check if our logistics sector is outperforming the national economy."

###### üõ† The Comparison Logic:
* The GDP Anchor: We use official World Bank data for Azerbaijan's GDP to represent the "Speed of the Nation."
* Indexed Growth: By setting both National GDP and Transport Income to a base of 100 in the year 1995, we can see which "line" is climbing faster.

###### üß† The Hypothesis:
If the Transport Income Line stays above the GDP Line, it proves that Azerbaijan is successfully transforming into a logistics-based economy. It demonstrates that the transport sector is generating value at a rate that exceeds the general economic growth of the country.

# 1. Load and Clean the GDP Data
# We reload it to ensure we have a clean copy for merging
gdp_df = pd.read_csv('Azerbaijan-GDP-GDP-US-2026-01-31-00-38.csv')

# Standardize columns and index
gdp_df.columns = ['Year', 'GDP_USD']
gdp_df['Year'] = pd.to_numeric(gdp_df['Year'], errors='coerce') # Handle any potential bad data
gdp_df = gdp_df.dropna().astype(int)
gdp_df.set_index('Year', inplace=True)

# 2. Merge GDP into the Main Income Dataset
# We use .join() which automatically aligns based on the 'Year' index
# This is safer than direct assignment if years are missing in one file
comparison_df = my_dataset[['Total (thousand dollars)']].join(gdp_df, how='inner')

# 3. Calculate Normalized Index (Base Year 1995 = 100)
# This formula answers: "If 1995 was 100, what is the value today?"
base_year = 1995
comparison_df['Income_Index'] = (comparison_df['Total (thousand dollars)'] / comparison_df.loc[base_year, 'Total (thousand dollars)']) * 100
comparison_df['GDP_Index'] = (comparison_df['GDP_USD'] / comparison_df.loc[base_year, 'GDP_USD']) * 100

# 4. Plot the "Efficiency Audit"
plt.figure(figsize=(12, 6))

# Transport Income Line (Green for Money/Growth)
